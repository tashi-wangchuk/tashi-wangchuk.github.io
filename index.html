Hi there,
I'm Tashi.

Welcome to my personal space
on the web.

I work as a teacher-researcher at Druk Gyalpo's Institute, where my interests sit at the intersection of AI, physics, and education. Teaching is something I care about deeply: I spend considerable time preparing each lecture, working through the underlying ideas until I understand them well enough to make them clear to students.

At heart, I am a physicist. I completed my undergraduate degree in Mathematics–Physics at the University of New Brunswick, Canada, where I had the good fortune to work at the UNB MRI Research Centre under the supervision of Bruce Balcom. You can find my CV here.

More recently, I have been drawn into quantum computing, a field I hope to explore much further in the months ahead. Alongside this, the Royal Academy Observatory at Druk Gyalpo's Institute, part of the PANOPTES network, has pulled me into astronomy almost by accident, and I hope to build something meaningful there with my students.

I live in Pangbisa, Paro. Outside of work, I read, work out, and spend a fair amount of time talking to Claude.
